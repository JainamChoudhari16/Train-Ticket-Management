Railway Facts

Extensive Network

The Indian Railways operates over 22,000 trains daily, covering more than 67,000 kilometers of track and connecting over 7,300 stations across the country.

Passenger Volume

Indian Railways transports over 23 million passengers daily, making it one of the world's largest railway networks serving approximately 8 billion people annually.

Heritage & History

India's first passenger train ran from Mumbai to Thane on April 16, 1853. Today, the network includes UNESCO World Heritage sites like the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway and Nilgiri Mountain Railway.
